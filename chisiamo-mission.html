About us

Our mission is to promote and develop societal awareness of and attention to transparency and accountability, be they related to the society-at-large or otherwise. Our goal is to increase citizens' involvement in the management of matters of public interest and to boost active participation of workers and employees to the correct management of corporations and companies they work for.

Accordingly, the Hermes Center for Transparency and Digital Human Rights' mission includes;

  • Supporting and developing free software with the purpose of increasing Freedom of Speech online and the protection of rights and personal freedom in a connected world in general;
  • Supporting and developing the whistle blowing free software solution named “GlobaLeaks” aimed at private and public organizations which shall be free to adopt the said software for any initiative they see fit;
  • Taking part in associations or organizations who share the same goals as the Hermes Center for Transparency and Digital Human Rights in the field of promotion and protection of civil liberties, anonymity in a connected world, whistle blowing, freedom of speech and investigative journalism;
  • Supporting the development and promotion of the Tor2web project and of the TOR network and related tools in general;
  • Supporting the development and ongoing management of the LeakDirectory website, listing whistleblowing websites;
  • Hosting or attending events with the aim of increasing the culture of transparency (i.e., public-interest whistle blowing, OpenData, OpenGovernment and any and all related topics)

In no event the Hermes Center for Transparency and Digital Human Rights shall gather and/or solicit secret or sensitive information, while it reserves the right to evaluate whether to give technical support to entities which are involved in such activities, provided that the Board so decides.

The Hermes Center for Transparency and Digital Human Rights, in order to pursue its goals, may decide to apply for membership in other organizations and/or to co-operate with entities whose mission does not conflict with the goals of the Hermes Center for Transparency and Digital Human Rights.
